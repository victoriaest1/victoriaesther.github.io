Research insights

What users were really struggling with

After 12 one-hour interviews and 6 recorded sessions, three clear themes emerged across all user groups.

😵
Information overload
Users reported feeling overwhelmed by the sheer number of metrics on the main screen. "I don't know where to look first" was cited in 9 of 12 interviews.
Pain point #1
🔍
Navigation confusion
Key actions were buried 3–4 levels deep. Users spent an average of 47 seconds finding features they used daily.
Pain point #2
📱
Mobile experience missing
40% of users accessed the product on mobile but the layout was desktop-only — tables overflowed, CTAs were unreachable.
Pain point #3
"
I come in every morning and it takes me 15 minutes to find the two things I actually need. The rest is just noise.
— Operations Manager, interviewed user · 7 years using the product
Design evolution

From chaos to clarity

The redesign focused on progressive disclosure — surface what matters, hide what doesn't, and make the critical path impossible to miss.

Reflections

What I'd do differently

💡
Involve engineers earlier. The tilt-on-hover micro-interactions I designed had to be simplified because I hadn't validated feasibility. Next time: co-design session in week 1 with dev.
🧪
Test at lower fidelity. I went to high-fi too fast and users commented on visual polish rather than usability. Low-fi paper prototype round first would have caught navigation issues cheaper.
📊
Define success metrics upfront. I added measurement retroactively. Starting with clear KPIs would have shaped the research questions and made the impact story tighter.
