Business context

The problem this solved

Finance teams were spending 12+ hours a week manually compiling reports from 3 different systems. Decisions were delayed and data was inconsistent.

⏱️
12 hrs/week lost
Manual data extraction from separate systems — Excel, the ERP, and a legacy CRM — combined every Monday morning by one analyst.
🔀
Data inconsistency
Numbers differed between departments because each team used a slightly different version of "the truth." No single source of record existed.
📉
Delayed decisions
Weekly reports were often 2–3 days stale by the time stakeholders saw them. Fast-moving situations couldn't be reacted to in time.
Data architecture

Four sources, one clean pipeline

Built a SQL-based ETL pipeline to unify four data sources into a single star-schema data model, refreshed nightly.

Methodology

How the project unfolded

📋
Week 1–2
Discovery & requirements gathering
Stakeholder interviews with finance, operations, and leadership. Mapped all existing reports and their consumers. Prioritised the top 12 KPIs that drove decisions.
InterviewsExcelMiro
🛠️
Week 3–4
Data modelling & ETL pipeline
Designed a star schema with 4 dimension tables and 1 fact table. Wrote SQL views to standardise data from all sources. Automated nightly refresh via scheduled job.
SQL ServerSSMSPython
📊
Week 5–6
Dashboard design & build
Iterated through 3 layout prototypes with stakeholders. Built final dashboard in Power BI with cross-report drill-through, bookmarks, and mobile layout.
Power BITableauDAX
🚀
Week 7
Deployment & training
Deployed to Power BI Service with row-level security per department. Ran 3 training sessions. Created documentation wiki for ongoing maintenance.
