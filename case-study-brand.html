The Challenge

A full brand in 14 days

A pre-seed startup was approaching their first investor demo with no visual identity — just a product name and a pitch deck. They needed a logo, a colour system, typography, and UI components in two weeks, or the demo would look amateurish against their competition.

The challenge wasn't just speed — it was longevity. The brand needed to feel considered and scalable, not like a rushed template. It had to hold up in a boardroom on day one and still feel right when the company was three times larger.

The Solution

Token-first, endlessly flexible

Every visual decision — colour, spacing, type scale, border radius — was built as a design token first, then applied to components. This means the entire brand can be recoloured or restyled in under an hour if the startup pivots or rebrands in a later funding round.

The Figma component library was built with auto-layout throughout, so every component resizes correctly regardless of content length. The brand guidelines PDF was written to be used by developers and marketers, not just designers — plain language, clear examples, no jargon.

Reflections

What I'd do differently

Stakeholder alignment on direction one is everything. Getting a clear "yes" or "no" on the first concept saves endless revision cycles. I'd now run a structured 30-minute direction review before any further work.
Build dark mode tokens from the start. Even if dark mode isn't needed immediately, retrofitting it later is painful. Adding it to the token structure on day one costs almost nothing.
